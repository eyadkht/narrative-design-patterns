Exploration

Filters and options let the reader steer the story themselves.

Why use this pattern?

To give the reader the highest degree of engagement, let them set the camera, choose what to compare, and discover findings on their own terms.

How to use it?

Enable readers to view and interact with the story through different filters and options. Keep the controls flat and discoverable; never bury the most useful filter under a menu.

12

Motion

Three durations, one easing curve. Anything longer than --dur-slow needs justification. Respects prefers-reduced-motion.

--dur-fast · 120ms

Hover lifts, button presses, micro-feedback.

--dur · 240ms

Default, card hover, chip activation, page transitions.

--dur-slow · 600ms

FLIP transitions, layer reveals, narrative pacing.

Easing, cubic-bezier(.2,.6,.2,1) · settled, slight overshoot avoided.

13

Accessibility

Rules the design system has to keep itself honest.

  • · Focus ring, 2px ink ring with 2px paper offset. Visible on every interactive element.
  • · Min target, 36×36px on touch, 24px hit-area minimum for inline controls.
  • · Color never alone, every category color is paired with a label (chip text, tag text) so colorblind users aren't excluded.
  • · Yellow is background only, never used for foreground text or thin strokes on white (fails WCAG).
  • · prefers-reduced-motion, all --dur-* tokens drop to 0ms automatically.
  • · Modal focus trap, when open, tab cycles inside; ESC closes; close button receives initial focus.
