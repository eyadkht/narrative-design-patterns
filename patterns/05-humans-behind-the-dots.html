Pattern 05 Engagement Emotion

Humans Behind the Dots

Surface the individual story hiding inside aggregate data to rekindle empathy at scale.

Why it works

Statistical compassion fatigue is real, the bigger the number, the harder it is to feel anything. One vivid personal detail does more emotional work than a thousand-row dataset. Dots make the scale; vignettes make it matter.

How to use it

Render the dataset as a grid of dots. Hover, focus, or tap reveals a one-sentence vignette about the person that dot represents. Keep vignettes specific and brief, a name, an age, a single image.

Live demo

Every weekday, 1.4 million people commute by train across the UK, the highest rate in Europe. Below is 100 of them on a single Tuesday-morning service into London. Each dot is one person; the colours show their age group, in the same proportions as the real UK rail commuter population (ORR National Rail Travel Survey).

Real-world example

Periscopic, US Gun Deaths every victim is rendered as an arc and a name. The animation builds the aggregate slowly, but each dot remains hoverable. You leave with the total and a handful of specific lives that did not finish.
