Pattern 13 Argumentation

Compare

Two things, side-by-side. The disparity is the chart.

Why it works

A single number has no scale. "$80 million" is meaningless until you see what it sits next to. Put a second number beside it, drawn at the same scale, and the gap argues for itself, no paragraph of explanation needed.

How to use it

Pick two things that share a unit, dollars, hours, lives. Draw them side by side, at the same scale. Don't break the axis or rescale to make the smaller one look bigger. If one bar is a sliver next to the other, that's the point.

Live demo

Three numbers you've heard. Three you probably haven't.

Each pair shares a dimension. Both bars are drawn to the same scale.

Real-world example

David McCandless's Billion Dollar-o-Gram puts wildly different dollar figures (defense budgets, charity costs, corporate profits) on the same plane. No analysis needed, seeing the rectangles side by side does the work.
