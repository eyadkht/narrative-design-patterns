Pattern 04 Engagement Emotion

Users Find Themselves

Invite readers to locate themselves in the data so the story becomes personally relevant.

Why it works

Abstract population data feels remote, numbers about other people. The moment readers see their own dot among the crowd, the story becomes about them. Engagement and recall spike because the data is no longer hypothetical.

How to use it

Add two or three dropdowns (age, region, income bracket) that reposition a highlighted dot in the population scatter. Animate the transition so the reader can track where they land. Follow it with a one-line contextual observation.

Live demo

70 workers across three regions, plotted by weekly hours against life satisfaction. Tell us about yourself, and find your dot.

Real-world example

NYT Upshot, You Draw It: Family Income and College readers drew their guess before the real curve was revealed. The act of placing themselves in the data made the gap between perception and reality feel visceral.
