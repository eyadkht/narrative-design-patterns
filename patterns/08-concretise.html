Pattern 08 Emotion

Concretise

Translate abstract quantities into familiar physical equivalents so the scale finally clicks.

Why it works

"Five million tonnes" is a phrase the brain can read but not feel. "Five Wembley Stadiums filled to the rim" is the same number with a body attached, something the eye can measure against memory. Concrete anchors make magnitude visceral.

How to use it

Pick a unit the reader already has a picture of, buses, pools, football pitches, double-decker stacks. Let the unit auto-switch as the magnitude grows. Show two or three alternative equivalents alongside so different mental models all get an anchor.

Live demo

The UK throws away roughly 5 million tonnes of plastic each year, a number too big to feel. Drag the slider to dial up a quantity, from one household's bin to the country's annual total, and watch it become something you can picture.
