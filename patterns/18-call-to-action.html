Pattern 18 Engagement

Call-to-action

End with a clear next step. Comprehension is fine. Intent is better. The reader who finishes your chart should know exactly what to do.

Why it works

Readers absorb a great chart, nod, and close the tab. A clear ask — vote, donate, sign, share, try it — turns that nod into a small commitment. The closer the action sits to the moment of understanding, the more likely the reader follows through.

How to use it

Pick one specific action, low-friction enough to do now. Place it at the end, in language that names what happens next (“register in 60 seconds”, not “learn more”). Show that others have already done it — social proof closes the deal.

Live demo — the CTA for this whole project

Most of the web is about to be written by machines — and readers are tuning out.

Reader enthusiasm for AI-generated creator content fell from 60% in 2023 to just 26% in 2025. On YouTube, content perceived as “AI slop” shows roughly 70% lower retention than human-fronted videos in the same niche.

Real-world example

The most-shared data-viz of the last decade, FT’s COVID-19 tracker , ended every page with the same line: share this chart, follow the data. Tens of thousands did. Comprehension turned into spread — the CTA did most of the work.
