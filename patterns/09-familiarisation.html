Pattern 09 Emotion Framing

Familiarisation

Re-label the chart around the reader's own reference point so unfamiliar scales feel intuitive.

Why it works

Readers can't feel the distance from London to Tokyo. But they can feel the distance from home to the supermarket, and they know it in their bones. Re-expressing big numbers in units the reader already carries around makes scale intuitive rather than abstract.

How to use it

Let the reader supply a personal anchor, their city, their commute, their salary, their height. Then re-label the chart's axis in that unit, in real time. Keep the original unit visible as a small caption so the conversion is transparent.

Real-world example
