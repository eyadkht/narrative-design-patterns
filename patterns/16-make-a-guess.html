Pattern 16 Framing Engagement

Make-a-guess

Make the reader draw their prediction before revealing the data. The gap between their line and the real one is the chart.

Why it works

A chart shown cold gets skimmed. The same chart shown after the reader has drawn their own prediction lands differently, there's a small commitment, a flicker of embarrassment, and a real "huh, I didn't know that" baked in by the time the real line appears.

How to use it

Start with an empty chart, axes only, no data. Give the reader one known point as an anchor and ask them to draw what they think happened next. On reveal, animate the real line over their guess and highlight the gap. Works best when the data is genuinely counter-intuitive.

Real-world example

The NYT's You Draw It: Family Income and College Chances is the canonical version of this pattern, the reader sketches their guess for how college attendance scales with family income, and the real curve materialises over the top. The shape of the gap, not the data itself, is the takeaway.
